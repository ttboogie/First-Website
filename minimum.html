What is the Minimum Wage?


The minimum wage is the legal minimum that a worker can be paid. It is the mandatory pierce floor. The minimum wage was implemented to create a minimum standard of living and stabilize the economy after the Great Depression.

Comparing the Living Wage to the Minimum Wage


Living wage and minimum wage are very similar. Originally, the government created minimum wage to be the living wage, but they have been found to be different. The biggest difference between the two is that living wage is based on costs to live and minimum wage is an amount set by law. When the government decides the minimum wage they consider the needs of businesses and their workers. Also the impact on the economy must be taken into consideration. With living wage the exact number is based on the factors that make up what is needed to live. Right now people that make minimum wage can not afford health insurance. This is one of the strong arguments for raising pay.
